# Step7C-real-D Edit Signal Forensics and Slot Scoring Calibration

## Scope

Step7C-real-D is a sidecar-only forensic analysis. It compares Step7C-real-B metric-directed single/edit candidates with Step7C-real-C small-window repack candidates. It does **not** integrate with the contest runtime solver, does **not** change finalizer semantics, does **not** start multi-iteration search, does **not** add another generator, and does **not** train or use RL.

The purpose is to explain why Step7C-real-B produced more official-like improvements than Step7C-real-C and to choose the next implementation direction.

- Step7C-real-B: 64 candidates, 3 official-like improvements.
- Step7C-real-C: 48 candidates, 1 official-like improvement.
- Official-like improving strategies:
  - `slack_fit_insertion`: 2

- B HPWL-gain conversion: 3 / 9.
- C HPWL-gain conversion: 1 / 7.

- Soft delta vs official-like delta Pearson: 0.948.

## Interpretation

The common pattern among official-like winners is narrow and clear: a local one-block HPWL/slack-fit move that does not increase bbox area or soft constraints. Step7C-real-C recovered one of these wins through its `critical_net_slack_fit_window` lane, but window repack added more noisy candidates and reduced official-like improvement density relative to Step7C-real-B.

The strongest cancellation evidence is not local-route safety; safety remains healthy. The bottleneck is metric quality:

- HPWL improvements often do not survive official-like cost once bbox/soft penalties are considered.
- Bbox and soft deltas are strongly positively correlated with worse official-like cost.
- Macro/regional candidates are preserved and non-global, but none are official-like improving in B/C.
- C's window-specific failures are mostly poor-target selection or metric tradeoff, not route collapse or legalizer failure.

## Decision

```text
expand_slack_fit_local_lane
```

Reason: B's local winner pattern is clearer and denser than C's window repack pattern. The next implementation should expand the slack-fit / HPWL-directed local lane with stricter bbox/soft-neutral scoring and more candidate coverage before adding larger windows, coarse region planning, or macro-closure generation.

## Next implementation direction

Recommended next step:

1. Return to the best Step7C-real-B local lanes: `slack_fit_insertion` and `hpwl_directed_local_nudge`.
2. Expand candidate coverage per case, but keep Step7G routing as the safety layer.
3. Add hard preselection filters that favor:
   - local route prediction;
   - one-block or very small changed-block count;
   - HPWL reduction;
   - zero bbox-area increase;
   - zero soft-constraint increase;
   - sufficient feasible local slack.
4. Keep original-inclusive Pareto reporting and continue reporting regional/macro/global candidates rather than silently dropping them.

Do **not** promote to multi-iteration Step7C yet. Do **not** build a coarse region planner or macro closure generator until local slack-fit expansion is exhausted or disproven.
